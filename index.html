What is a Flock camera, actually?

Flock Safety sells pole-mounted cameras to police departments, sheriffs, HOAs and businesses across NC. Every vehicle that passes is photographed and logged — plate, make, model, color, even bumper stickers and roof racks — into a searchable, shareable database.

No crime has to be suspected. The record is built anyway, kept for up to 90 days under NC law, and can be shared with other agencies — including out-of-state and federal ones.

And it's no longer just plate readers: Flock now sells always-on microphones, AI PTZ cameras that follow people, and drones that read plates from the air — all feeding the same database.

WHY IT MATTERS IN NC
  • Your daily movements — home, work, church, clinic — become a permanent, searchable log.
  • NC's State Bureau of Investigation highway pilot has grown to roughly 140 cameras across 32 agencies and logged 150+ million plate scans.
  • The SBI's highway plate-reader pilot was set to expire in July 2026 — but lawmakers locked ALPRs onto NC highways for good, folding the authority into the 2026 state budget after a standalone bill (HB 206) stalled.
  • Wrongful stops have already happened — Fayetteville paid a $60,000 settlement after Jacqueline McNeill was handcuffed over a plate misread, and Jasmine Horne was wrongfully arrested the same way. Read the cases →

NC communities are already pushing back

Nationwide, 82 Flock contracts were cancelled across 28 states between 2021 and mid-2026 — 39 of them in the first five months of 2026 alone. North Carolina is part of that wave.

Voted 5–0 to defund $47K in cameras (July 2026)
