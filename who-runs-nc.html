WHO HOLDS THE POWER

The people who decide NC's surveillance future

ALPR policy in North Carolina runs through a divided government: a Democratic Governor and Council of State, and a Republican-led General Assembly that controls which bills move. Knowing who does what tells you exactly where to apply pressure.

The Executive Branch

Signs or vetoes legislation, directs state agencies (including the SBI and NCDOT), and sets enforcement priorities.

Governor
Josh Stein
76th Governor (since 2025), former NC Attorney General. Signs or vetoes ALPR-related bills; oversees the Dept. of Public Safety and SBI.

The state's top law-enforcement lawyer. The AG's office weighs in on data-sharing, records law, and civil-liberties questions around ALPRs.

The General Assembly

This is where ALPR rules are written — or blocked. Republicans hold majorities in both chambers, so leadership decides which bills get a hearing. The 2025–26 sessions saw ALPR provisions (HB 206, HB 372) added, debated, and stripped out, before the SBI program was folded into the state budget.

Senate — President Pro Tempore
Phil Berger
The most powerful figure in the NC Senate; controls what legislation reaches the floor. District 26 (Rockingham).
Find in NC Senate directory →
House — Speaker
Destin Hall
Sets the NC House agenda and committee assignments. District 87 (Caldwell/Watauga).
Find in NC House directory →
Why leadership matters more than any single vote: in NC, the Pro Tem and the Speaker decide whether a bill is even heard. A privacy bill with broad support can die simply by never being scheduled — so respectful, persistent pressure on leadership and committee chairs is often the highest-leverage move.

THE FULL CHAIN OF DECISION-MAKERS
  • Your NC House & Senate members — vote on statewide ALPR law. Look them up.
  • Your city council / county commissioners — approve or cancel the local Flock contract. This is often the fastest path to a win.
  • Your sheriff & police chief — decide whether to buy in, and set the local data policy.
  • School boards & HOAs — increasingly signing their own ALPR deals.

Local officials move fastest

Every NC community that has dropped Flock — Macon, Hillsborough, Pittsboro, Chatham — did it at the local level, through county commissioners and town councils. You don't have to wait on the legislature. Show up at a meeting near you.
